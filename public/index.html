Growth in Motion: From Margins to Code

As a justice-impacted individual, I found myself navigating life outside the usual boundaries of society—sharing space, ideas, and survival with people from every walk of life. That experience shaped how I connect, adapt, and solve problems, often in unconventional ways.

During that time, I was fortunate to be accepted into The Last Mile, a transformative coding program that introduced me to web development and sparked my love for tech. Over the course of a year, I learned the foundations of the MERN stack—MongoDB, Express, and Node. js—though the program concluded just before we reached React.

Now, on the other side of that experience, I’m continuing my journey through Code the Dream, deepening my skills and preparing to take on full-stack challenges. In the meantime, I’m building with what I call the “Basic Stack”—HTML, CSS, and JavaScript—to create my first portfolio projects and demonstrate growth in real time.

This portfolio, as it stands, is intentionally minimal. It reflects where I am right now, not where I’m going—but it's the seed of something bigger. My goal is to keep it honest, evolving, and open to community input as I grow into the developer I know I can be.
